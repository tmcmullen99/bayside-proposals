Our quality standards

Here's what your project actually goes through, from the day we walk your yard to the day we hand you the keys. Tap any phase to see the detail.

01 Site preparation Where every long-lasting hardscape begins ›

Before a single paver lands, we set the stage. Our crew excavates to the right depth for your soil type and project category — patios, driveways, and pool decks each have their own minimum specs. We mark utilities, set up edge lines, and confirm grading so water flows away from your house, not toward it.

This is the unglamorous part of the project, but it's the difference between a hardscape that looks great for 5 years and one that looks great for 30. Most failures we get called in to fix come from someone skipping this phase.

02 Base & drainage The foundation under your finished surface ›

The foundation is built in lifts of compacted aggregate — typically 2 to 4 inches at a time — to the standard your project demands. Patios get at least 4 inches; driveways get 8 inches; pool decks and parking get more. Every lift is compacted with the right equipment so the base reaches 98% Standard Proctor density before we move on.

Drainage is engineered in at this phase, not bolted on later. Surfaces are sloped a minimum of 2% away from structures, and where the project calls for it, perforated pipe and free-draining backfill carry water out before it can pool, freeze, or heave.

03 Surface installation Pavers, porcelain, and finish work ›

Concrete pavers go down on a precisely screeded one-inch bedding-sand layer, with edge restraints staked into the compacted base. Joints get tight tolerances (1/16 to 3/16 inch) and proper polymeric jointing sand to lock everything together and resist weed and insect intrusion.

Porcelain is a different beast — slabs are heavier, more brittle, and require pre-compacted bedding sand and 4mm plastic spacers between every unit. Porcelain is never compacted with a plate compactor after install (a common mistake that breaks slabs); we hand-set, level, and verify alignment instead.

04 Walls & vertical features Retaining walls, fire features, seat walls ›

Retaining walls are engineered, not stacked. We start with a leveling pad of compacted aggregate, lay the first course slightly below grade for stability, and build up using running-bond patterns with proper backfill drainage and (where required) geosynthetic reinforcement layers based on soil type and wall height.

Fire features and seat walls get the same care — proper bases, weather-resistant adhesives where they belong, gas lines run by licensed plumbers when applicable, and finish caps mortared in to last.

05 Finishing & warranty Polymeric sand, sealing, and what we stand behind ›

The last day is when most homeowners notice everything coming together. Polymeric jointing sand goes in, gets activated with water, and locks every joint. Where the design calls for sealing, we use the right sealer for the surface — penetrating for natural stone, joint-stabilizing for concrete pavers, none for some porcelain — and apply only after the install has fully cured.

Belgard's transferable lifetime limited warranty covers the materials, and we stand behind the workmanship. Adherence to ICPI (Interlocking Concrete Paver Institute) standards isn't optional for us — it's how we keep that warranty intact and how we sleep at night.
